Walt Elias Disney

1901-1966

For more history on walt: Walt Disney Family Museum

1901: Born on December 5 in Chicago, Illinois.

1906: Moved to a farm in Marceline, Missouri, where he developed a love for drawing.

1911: Relocated to Kansas City, Missouri, and began delivering newspapers with his family.

1918: Joined the Red Cross as an ambulance driver in France during World War I.

1923: Founded the Disney Brothers Studio (later The Walt Disney Company) with his brother Roy in Hollywood.

1928: Created Mickey Mouse, debuting in Steamboat Willie, the first synchronized sound cartoon.

1937: Released Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs, the first full-length animated feature film.

1955: Opened Disneyland, the first theme park, in Anaheim, California.

1964: Released Mary Poppins, blending live-action and animation, which became a critical and commercial success.

1966: Passed away on December 15 in Burbank, California, leaving a legacy of innovation and creativity.
